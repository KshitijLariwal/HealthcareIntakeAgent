# Preliminary Triage Guidelines for General Intake

## Abdominal Pain
If a patient reports severe, sudden-onset abdominal pain, especially localized to the lower right quadrant, this may indicate appendicitis. Ask if the pain is accompanied by fever, nausea, or vomiting. Do not diagnose, but flag for immediate physician review.

## Respiratory Issues
For patients reporting shortness of breath or persistent cough lasting more than two weeks, inquire about chest pain, fever, or a history of smoking. 

## Headaches
For persistent headaches, ask the patient about the onset (sudden or gradual), severity on a scale of 1-10, and if they are experiencing any visual disturbances or sensitivity to light.
